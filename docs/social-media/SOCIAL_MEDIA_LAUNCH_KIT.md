# KiNDD Social Media Launch Kit

Everything needed to stand up KiNDD's social presence: which platforms to
claim, ready-to-paste profile copy, correctly sized profile and cover images,
and a starter content plan. All copy and imagery use the KiNDD brand
(kinddhelp.com), the brand palette, and the existing logo.

## What this kit does and does not do

This kit prepares every asset and instruction so that creating each account is
a short, click-through task for you.

It does not, and cannot, create the live accounts themselves. Facebook,
Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube, and X all require signing up as you: a
verified email or phone number, SMS confirmation codes, and acceptance of each
platform's terms. Those steps must be completed by a person signed in with the
brand's own credentials. Follow the per-platform steps below and you will have
each profile live in a few minutes using the assets in `assets/`.

## Recommended platforms (tiered)

The audience is Los Angeles County families and caregivers navigating
neurodevelopmental disorder (NDD) and developmental-disability services, plus
the clinicians, regional centers, and nonprofit partners who serve them.

| Tier | Platform | Why | Effort |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Launch now | Facebook Page | Where LA parent and caregiver groups already share local resources; strong for older caregivers and community reshares. | Low |
| Launch now | Instagram | Primary consumer channel for parents and caregivers; visual, local, links to the app. | Medium |
| Launch now | LinkedIn Page | Credibility with clinicians, regional centers, nonprofits, and grant/partner audiences. | Low |
| Phase 2 | TikTok | Excellent organic reach for short "how do I..." explainers, but needs a steady video cadence to be worth it. | High |
| Phase 2 | YouTube | Home for longer explainers; embeds on the site and helps search. | Medium |
| Optional | X / Twitter | Lower payoff for this audience; claim the handle to protect the name even if you post rarely. | Low |

Recommendation: launch Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn first with a shared
content calendar. Add TikTok and YouTube once there is a repeatable way to
produce short video. Claim the X handle defensively.

## Handles

Claim the same handle everywhere for consistency. Preferred, in order:

1. `kinddhelp` (matches the domain)
2. `kindd.help`
3. `kinddresources`

Reserve the name on every platform in Tier 1 and 2 the same day, even the ones
you will not post to yet, so the handle is not taken by someone else. Record
which handle you secured on each platform in the tracking table at the end.

## Step-by-step account creation

Do these once, in order. Budget about 10 minutes per platform.

### Before you start
1. Decide the owner login. Use a shared brand email such as
   support@kinddhelp.com or a dedicated social@ address, not a personal
   account, so the pages are not tied to one individual.
2. Have a phone number ready for SMS verification.
3. Download the seven files from `docs/social-media/assets/` to the device you
   will use.

### Facebook Page
1. Log in to the brand Facebook account (create a personal account first if
   there is none; a Page must be attached to a personal profile as admin).
2. Go to Pages, then Create new Page.
3. Page name: `KiNDD - NDD Resource Navigator`. Category: Nonprofit
   Organization. Bio: paste the short description above.
4. Upload profile photo `avatar-heart-white.png` and cover `facebook-cover.png`.
5. Set username to `@kinddhelp` under Settings.
6. Add website, email, and the "Learn more" action button.
7. Publish, then pin an intro post (see starter posts below).

### Instagram
1. In the app, create a new account with the brand email.
2. Switch to a Professional account: Settings, Account type, Switch to
   Professional, choose a Health/Community category.
3. Set username `@kinddhelp` and name `KiNDD - NDD Resources`.
4. Upload `avatar-heart-white.png`, paste the bio, add the kinddhelp.com link.
5. Link to the Facebook Page: Settings, Accounts Center, add the Page. This
   enables cross-posting.

### LinkedIn Page
1. From a personal LinkedIn account, choose For Business, Create a Company Page.
2. Enter name, `kinddhelp` public URL, website, industry, tagline, and About.
3. Upload logo `avatar-lockup-white.png` and cover `linkedin-company-cover.png`.
4. Publish, then invite relevant connections and partners to follow.

### TikTok (Phase 2)
1. Sign up with the brand email, set username `@kinddhelp`.
2. Switch to a Business account: Settings, Account, Switch to Business Account,
   category Education or Health.
3. Upload `avatar-heart-white.png`, paste the bio, add the website when eligible.

### YouTube (Phase 2)
1. Sign in with the brand Google account, create a Channel (use a Brand
   Account so it is not tied to a person).
2. Set the name and `@kinddhelp` handle, add the description.
3. Upload `avatar-heart-white.png` as the picture and `youtube-banner.png` as
   channel art.

### X / Twitter (optional)
1. Sign up with the brand email, set username `@kinddhelp`.
2. Upload `avatar-heart-white.png` and header `twitter-header.png`, paste the bio.

## Starter content: first two weeks

Keep a consistent, useful voice: clear, warm, practical, never clinical advice.
Every post should help a family take one concrete next step. Post two to three
times a week to start. These map cleanly to Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn;
trim for length per platform.

1. Introduction / pinned post
   "KiNDD is a free tool that helps Los Angeles County families find
   neurodevelopmental services. Enter your ZIP code and see your regional
   center, its service area, and verified providers near you. Start at
   kinddhelp.com."
2. What is a regional center
   "LA County has 7 regional centers, and which one serves you depends on where
   you live. KiNDD matches your ZIP code to the right one and shows its service
   area on a map. Find yours at kinddhelp.com."
3. Feature spotlight: the map
   "Filter 600+ verified providers by therapy type, ages served, insurance, and
   distance. Tap a provider to call or get directions. Free at kinddhelp.com."
4. Next-step guidance
   "Just received a diagnosis and not sure what comes next? KiNDD suggests your
   next step, from requesting an intake evaluation to preparing for an
   appointment. Ask KiNDD at kinddhelp.com."
5. Bilingual support
   "KiNDD answers questions in English and Spanish, so families can navigate
   services in the language they are most comfortable in."
6. Provider or partner shout-out
   Highlight a regional center program or a provider type (ABA, speech,
   occupational, physical therapy) and how to find it on the map.

Content principles:
- Lead with the family's problem, then the concrete next step.
- Always include the link (kinddhelp.com) or a clear call to action.
- No medical advice. Keep to navigation, eligibility basics, and how to use the
  tool.
- Reuse the brand colors and logo so posts are recognizable at a glance.

## Cross-posting and scheduling

To post once and publish to several platforms:
- Facebook and Instagram cross-post natively through Meta Business Suite (free).
- For all platforms in one place, a scheduler such as Buffer or Later (both have
  free tiers) can queue posts to Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, and X.
- Draft a week of posts at a time from the starter list above and schedule them.
